Jenny Cortez

Internal Director

  • Email: executivebridges@gmail.com

I’m currently a 4th year majoring in Ethnic Studies and minoring in Education. Being a first generation college student has cultivated a passion in me to do for others what was done for me. That includes being the bridges Internal Director for this 2019-2020 academic year, as well as being a college advisor in my hometown of Oakland. Through these roles I’ve been able to do work to demystify higher education for students from a similar background as me, as well as youth directly from my community. In my free time, I enjoy taking naps, watching youtube, and weight training (haven’t time for that in a while though).

Annabel Murillo

Organizing and Community Development Director

  • Email: organizingbridges@gmail.com

Hello community! My name is Annabel (she/her/ella) and I’m a 4th year studying Political Science and minoring in Public Policy. Coming into Berkeley as a first generation student was one of the hardest and most emotional times in my life and for my parents. I knew I was going to need help navigating this campus and was so happy for my Summer Bridge 2016 fam! From here I went on to learn about #Fight4Spaces organizing and was introduced to EOP, Raices and bridges, which have helped me at Berkeley since day one. My role as the Organizing and Community Development director for bridges is primarily focused on our #Fight4Spaces campaign directed in discussing and collaborating with administration, LGBTQ+ community, and RRCs to get permanent physical space on campus for current and future minority students. In my free time I love to go for a swim and am always down for a coffee chat so feel free to reach out!

Angela Lawenko

Retention Director

  • Email: retentionbridges@gmail.com

I’m a 4th year majoring in Integrative Biology. I’ve worked in organizations that provide resources to students of color such as EOP, Beyond12, APASD, and now bridges as Retention Director. My passion stems from my experiences of being a low-income, first generation, student of color. When I was a first year, I didn’t know how to navigate the rigor of classes and being homesick, thus I ended up on academic probation. I also often skipped meals because of financial constraints. Without the support of friends and bridges community connecting me to resources, I wouldn’t have been retained on this campus. I value empathy, love, respect, collaboration, intentionality, and sustainability.

Lien Truong

Program Director

  • Email: lien.truong@berkeley.edu
  • Office: LEAD Center 432 Eshleman Hall
  • Walk-In Office Hours: Monday 1-3pm, Thursdays 10am-12pm

Born and raised in (West) Oakland, California - She earned her undergraduate degree at Saint Mary’s College majoring in Sociology and Ethnic Studies. In 2013, she received her Master’s in Education with an concentration in Equity and Social Justice at San Francisco State University. She was the only student in her cohort who earned her degree in single year, in a two-year program.

She is the youngest of four, raised by a single mother who was a refugee from Vietnam. She is very close to her siblings who helped raised her when her father left when she was five years old. She is a first generation and low-income women of color.

She thrives off one-on-one interactions because that’s where she is able to learn the most about who you are, and what is that “one thing” you aim to strive for in life. She lives life with a purpose, and wants to inspire others be inspired in whatever makes them happy.

Her MBTI is I-S-T-J, and sometimes can be I-S-F-J. Her strengths' finder includes: significance, strategic, achiever, discipline, and connectedness. She is a Taurus; which she learned is important to note in the first two minutes of her interview with Carolyn Nguyen.

I am Lien (Le-In), and looking forward to developing and earning students’ trust, experience, and goals to create a better inclusive space that respects and values their passion to create change.

Lyone (lie-own) Conner (pronouns she/her/hers) joined the bridges family during summer 2016. She supported the staff and RRCs though many programs, including but not limited to POC Day of Resistance, Youth Mentorships, bGST, BACT, Incentive Award Review Committee, RRC & bridges retreats, SoCal outreach trips, banquets, and Senior & Transfer Weekend. Whew!

Because she resides in Martinez, if you see her on campus after hours, she'll probably have her littles (Tauvia 7 & Taun 4) with her. Lyone, who loves to dance, grew up in Sacramento, CA, also attended Saint Mary's College, like Lien, with a double major in Spanish and Liberal and Civic Studies (2004) and a Masters in Leadership (2011). As a first-gen, low-income, child of a single parent, Lyone was not exposed to social justice education as a student, nor told about the possibilities of college (applied to one school and was fortunate to get in), so she is grateful for the opportunity to be of support to bridges in this heartwork.

P.S. She's a Gemini (ahhh!!!)
