About Us

bridges Multicultural Resource Center is dedicated to the empowerment of underrepresented and underserved students through educational support and cross-cultural collaboration.

What is bridges?

bridges Multicultural Resource Center is a coalition of student-initiated and student-run Recruitment and Retention Centers, or RRC for short. RAZA (Chican@/Latin@ RRC), BRRC (Black RRC), PASS (Pilipino Academic Student Services), REACH! (Asian Pacific Islander RRC) and NARRC (Native American RRC) have been established prior to the formation of bridges. These RRCs existed due to a lack of underrepresented students on the UC Berkeley campus.

Following the passage of SP-1 and SP-2, two University policies that banned affirmative action procedures on the UC level, Proposition 209 was voted into effect in 1996 which effectively banned race, sex, and ethnicity consideration in public sectors. As a result, the work of the RRCs was illegal because the centers used race-based outreach which was banned as a result. These centers came together to form a multicultural coalition - bridges to circumnavigate the system and provide an arena for collaborative efforts.

Throughout the years, bridges has a rich sense of political activism at the University. We were the first student organization to repeal a UC-wide policy (SP-1). We have boycotted Senior Weekend, an outreach event aimed to recruit admitted underrepresented Cal students, which produced a drop of 50% in students of color. To date, bridges Senior Weekend has been the most successful yield event that the University has. bridges was a large stakeholder during the Bake Sale Protest and Occupy. In addition, we are currently involved in many crucial conversations regarding the state of diversity and equity on the UC Berkeley campus.

Mission Statement

To promote unity among the Recruitment and Retention Centers and other related organizations which provide educational services for the students in our communities. Through multicultural projects, bridges seeks to:
1. Empower underrepresented students to pursue post-secondary education opportunities.
2. Provide support for admitted and current underrepresented students.
3. Increase cross-cultural dialogue and facilitate collaboration.

    The Black Recruitment and Retention Center at UC Berkeley aims to recognize the struggle of young people of color, and our privilege as Black students in higher education. Native American Recruitment and Retention Center (NARRC)

  • Description
  • Organization Chart
  • Learn More

    NARRC is a student-initiated organization consisting of university faculty, staff, and undergraduate students who recognize the significance of keeping higher education accessible and attainable for Native Americans. We recognize the complexities of Native American histories and identities and work to convey this to the larger campus community. NARRC concentrates its recruitment efforts on diverse Native communities, encouraging Native Americans of all ages to apply and attend various forms of post-secondary education. We seek to increase Native presence at all levels of higher education, including but not limited to, community colleges, tribal colleges, four-year institutions, and technical schools. We promote community involvement beyond campus borders by hosting collaborative events with organizations such as the Native American Health Center, Intertribal Friendship House, and the American Indian Child Resource Center.

    NARRC works to create and to provide a variety of support programs and resources for Native American students at UC Berkeley in order to help them succeed and ultimately graduate. Our organization aims to establish a support system at Cal by providing safe spaces on campus as well as collaborating with other on-campus organizations. NARRC plays a key role in helping students balance individual success at Cal while maintaining pride in their heritage(s). Members of NARRC set an example for younger Native students, demonstrating that they can get here and also be successful in this competitive environment. It is our purpose to keep Native students at Cal and to increase and maintain adequate retention within the University. We must provide students with the necessary resources to achieve and foster a sense of community amongst our members.

Pilipino Academic Student Services (PASS)

  • Description
  • Organization Chart
  • Learn More

    Founded in 1985, Pilipino Academic Student Services (PASS) seeks to recruit and retain Pilipin@s into higher education. Recruitment events include Filipino Empowerment Day, outreaches toward under-resourced schools, SHADOW days for students to experience the Berkeley campus, and Youth Mentorship in San Francisco. Retention resources include study jams, mixers, the Pilipin@ Academic Student Orientation Class, and a gender and sexuality awareness component. Other components of PASS include community fundraising events for an AB540 scholarship and Friends of PASS that raises money for Senior Weekend. PASS facilitates internships for students who wish to become more familiar with navigating educational barriers for Pilipin@s into higher education. Ultimately, PASS is an a organization of mentorship, friendship, and community.

Chicana/Chicano Latina/Latino Recruitment and Retention Center (Raza)

  • Description
  • Organization Chart
  • Learn More

    UC Berkeley Chican@/Latin@ students established the RAZA Recruitment and Retention Center to increase the number of Chican@/Latin@ students in institutions of higher education and to provide academic and social support networks in order to encourage academic achievement among students.

    Since its establishment in 1976, in conjunction with the efforts and dedication of student volunteers and interns, the RAZA Recruitment and Retention Center continues its mission of proactively increasing the status of the Raza community professionally, socially and politically, both on and off campus.

    The RRRC’s goal is to increase the enrollment of Chican@/Latin@ high school students in institutions of higher education by sending UCB students on recruitment trips and outreaches to high schools, middle schools and community colleges. The Center also helps improve the retention rate of Chican@/Latin@s through various programs and events throughout the academic year.

Asian Pacific Islander Recruitment and Retention Center (REACH!)

  • Description
  • Organization Chart
  • Learn More

    Since 1994, REACH! has been committed to the service, empowerment, and mobilization of immigrant, refugee, and under served Asian/Pacific Islanders by promoting higher education to empower ourselves and challenge the economic and social inequalities facing our communities. While the UC Berkeley campus has 42% Asian Pacific Islander student population currently enrolled, REACH! strives to break the myth that we are the “minority without need” by serving the underserved and underrepresented Asian/Pacific Islanders that aren’t always given the resources they need. REACH! was not just going to increase the numbers of underrepresented Asians/Pacific Islanders at Berkeley but REACH! would be a training ground for future educators, activists, and leaders who would address society’s injustices through our life’s work. Thus, we created recruitment, mentorship, and retention programs to raise political awareness, build coalitions, develop youth leaders, and build leadership in ourselves.
